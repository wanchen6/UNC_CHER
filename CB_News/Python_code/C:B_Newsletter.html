A Qualitative Exploration of the Mental Health and Psychosocial Contexts of HIV-Positive Adolescents in Tanzania

Megan Ramaiya, Kristen Sullivan, et al.

The purpose of this study was to identify salient psychosocial and mental health challenges confronted by HIV-positive youth in a resource-poor Tanzanian setting. A total of 24 qualitative interviews were conducted with a convenience sample of adolescents aged 12-24 receiving outpatient HIV care at a medical center in Moshi, Tanzania. READ MORE...

Healing Without Waging War

Jing-Bao Nie, Adam Gilbertson, Malcolm de Roubaix, Ciara Staunton, Anton van Niekerk, Joseph D. Tucker, Stuart Rennie

In this article, philosophical analysis and an interdisciplinary literature review utilizing key texts from sociology, anthropology, history, and Chinese and African studies were conducted to investigate the current proliferation of military metaphors. We found the use of these metaphors to be ironic, unfortunate, and unnecessary.READ MORE...

Gender and the Science of Difference

Jill A. Fisher | 2011 | Rutgers University Press

This interdisciplinary volume presents empirical inquiries into today's science, including examples of gendered scientific inquiry and medical interventions and research. It analyzes how scientific and medical knowledge produces gender norms through an emphasis on sex differences, and includes both U.S. and non-U.S. cases and examples.READ FULL ARTICLE...

"Ready-to-recruit" or "ready-to-consent" populations?

Jill A. Fisher | 2007 | Qualitative inquiry: QI

This paper queries the pharmaceutical industry's concept of ''ready-to-recruit'' populations by examining its recruitment strategies for clinical trials and the types of human subjects who participate in these drug studies. The pharmaceutical industry has profited from a system comprised of what can more aptly be characterized as ready-to-consent populations, meaning populations who do not have better alternatives than participation in clinical trials. Direct to Consumer Responsibility

Jill A. Fisher, Lorna M. Ronald | 2008 | Patients, Consumers and Civil Society

This chapter explores the pharmaceutical industry's strategic utilization of empowerment discourse in two realms: direct-to-consumer advertising (DTCA) & clinical drug development. By analyzing findings from two empirical projects, this chapter is able to shed light on trends in the pharmaceutical industry's discourse about empowerment & consumption from the clinical testing to marketing of new drugs. Tracking the social dimensions of RFID systems in hospitals

Jill A. Fisher, Torin Monahan | 2008 | International Journal of Medical Informatics

Research involved qualitative methods including participant observation and interviews with hospital staff members and industry consultants in the United States.
